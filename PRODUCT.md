# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Colombian SMB owners (dueños de negocio) who either have no digital presence or have it without real strategy behind it. They are time-constrained business operators — not marketing specialists — who need a partner they can trust directly. They talk to the expert, not an account manager. The typical profile: a clinic owner in Bogotá, a construction services business in Medellín, a local retailer who is active on WhatsApp but invisible on search.

## Product Purpose

Position Colombian SMBs digitally, from zero to real presence, with a strategy built for their specific sector. Blaa Group builds what doesn't exist or fixes what exists without direction — social profiles, ad campaigns, SEO, email — always with the business owner as the direct counterpart. Fixed pricing, defined scope, no intermediaries. Success means the client gets measurable traction in a 30-day pilot, then chooses to continue because the results speak.

## Brand Personality

Bold, energetic, challenger. Blaa Group disrupts the typical Colombian agency cliché without swinging into flashy performance-agency territory. The voice is confident because the work backs it up — not because the site is loud. Speaks plainly, directly, and like someone who has done this for real businesses and knows what actually moves the needle.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS agency (WeFlex/Webflow showcase look)**: Oversaturated accent gradients, hero-metric templates (big number + small label), glassmorphism card decks, identical icon-heading-text grids. Feels like a template, not a conviction.
- **Traditional Colombian agency**: Heavy corporate blues, formal institutional language, PDF-style layout, no personality. Looks like it was built in 2015 and hasn't been touched since.
- **Flashy performance agency**: Claim-first copy ("we grow brands 10x"), neon accents on black, infinite logo scroll for social proof. Loud without being credible.
- **Cold editorial / portfolio**: Too abstract, too minimal. Looks like a design studio's portfolio. A dueño de pyme doesn't feel spoken to — it feels out of reach.

## References

- **Linear / Liveblocks**: Sharp, opinionated, product-confident. The site doesn't try to impress with decoration — the precision of the execution is the argument. Strong typographic hierarchy, restrained palette, every element earns its place. That's the tone.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the argument.** Don't announce expertise with big claims. Demonstrate it through how the site is structured, the precision of the copy, and the clarity of the offering.
2. **Speak to the dueño directly.** Language is plain, contextual, Colombian — without resorting to regional slang. The client is a business owner, not a marketing director. Every sentence should land with them.
3. **Challenger confidence, not challenger noise.** Bold doesn't mean loud. The site should feel authoritative the way a specialist feels authoritative — not because they shout, but because they clearly know more than anyone else in the room.
4. **Earned specificity.** The differentiator is sector depth, not general marketing expertise. Surface that specificity. Avoid the generic everywhere.
5. **Presence that converts.** This is a brand surface, but it must move people to contact. Design choices should serve the conversion path without looking like conversion-rate-optimization templates.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum throughout. 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, semantic HTML, keyboard navigable. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for scroll-driven animations (the site uses reveal directives). No accessibility is traded for visual effect.
